Most startup founders think design systems are a big-company problem. Stripe, Airbnb, and Google have them. You have a three-person team and a runway to extend — why would you invest in a design system now?

Here's the problem: by the time you *think* you need one, the damage is already done. Inconsistent buttons across your app. Five slightly different shades of your brand color. A developer rebuilding a component the designer already built in Figma — differently. A new hire who can't tell which is the "right" version.

The best time to build a design system is before you scale. The second best time is today.

## What a Design System Actually Is

A design system isn't just a component library. It's the combination of:

1. **Design tokens** — the raw values that define your visual language: colors, typography, spacing, border radius, shadows
2. **Component library** — reusable UI components built from those tokens
3. **Usage guidelines** — documentation on when and how to use each component
4. **Figma library** — the design-side implementation that keeps design and code in sync

When all four exist and stay synchronized, every team member — designer, developer, product manager — is working from the same source of truth.

## The Real Cost of Not Having One

### The Consistency Tax

Without a design system, every new page or feature requires decisions that should have been made once. "Should this button be 36px or 40px tall?" "Is this border radius 8px or 12px?" These micro-decisions seem small individually. They add up to hours of meetings, inconsistency in the product, and an interface that feels stitched together.

### The Onboarding Penalty

Every time you hire a designer or developer, they spend their first two weeks just learning the patterns — if those patterns are even documented. With a design system, onboarding is dramatically faster. New team members can be productive in hours, not weeks.

### The Rework Spiral

Without a system, components get rebuilt from scratch. A modal that already exists in three places gets built a fourth time, slightly differently. This compounds — every rebuild introduces new inconsistencies, which require more fixes.

## The Startup-Friendly Design System

You don't need Stripe's ten-person design systems team. You need:

- **One Figma file** with your component library
- **Design tokens defined** as Figma Variables (or a simple style guide)
- **Core components** — button, input, card, modal, navigation, table
- **A shared Figma team library** so the whole team uses the same components

That's it to start. You can expand over time.

## Signs You Need a Design System Now

- Your product has more than 5 screens or pages
- More than one person is working on design or development
- You've caught yourself saying "what's the right way to do X?" more than once
- Your Figma file has components that aren't in the code — or vice versa
- Onboarding a new team member to the design took more than a week
